Vegetable Overview

Vegetables are an essential part of a healthy diet, providing vitamins, minerals, and fiber. They help protect against chronic diseases and support energy levels and digestion. MyPlate recommends adults consume 2 to 3 cups of vegetables each day, including a variety of colors and types. Eating a mix of leafy greens, starchy vegetables, and colorful veggies ensures a balanced intake of nutrients.

Carrot

In Season: Spring & Fall

Carrots are rich in beta-carotene and vitamin A. They support eye health and immunity.

Broccoli

In Season: Fall & Winter

Broccoli contains fiber, vitamin C, and antioxidants that protect your heart and immune system.

Eggplant

In Season: Summer

Eggplants are low in calories and high in fiber, supporting digestion and heart health.

Spinach

In Season: Spring & Fall

Spinach is packed with iron, calcium, and vitamins that strengthen bones and boost immunity.

Bell Pepper

In Season: Summer & Fall

Bell peppers are high in vitamin C and antioxidants. They add color and crunch to your meals.

Cucumber

In Season: Summer

Cucumbers are hydrating and refreshing, rich in water and important minerals.
